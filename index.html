🧠 Unit-2 (KG) - Psychological Measurement & Assessment

1. Explain the Method of Equal Appearing Intervals.

  • a) Outline the steps involved in constructing a scale using this method.
  • b) Discuss its assumptions and major advantages.
  • c) Critically evaluate its limitations with suitable psychological examples.
🔗

2. Describe the Method of Summated Ratings (Likert Scale).

  • a) Explain the process of item selection, scoring, and validation.
  • b) Differentiate between summated and equal-appearing interval scales.
  • c) Evaluate the reliability and practical utility of the Likert approach.
🔗

3. Discuss the Method of Paired Comparisons.

  • a) Describe how stimuli are compared and ranked in this method.
  • b) Explain the mathematical basis used to derive the scale values.
  • c) Indicate potential areas in psychology where this method is most effective.
📝

4. Elaborate on the Method of Successive Categories.

  • a) Explain the conceptual framework and procedure of this scaling technique.
  • b) Compare it with the method of equal-appearing intervals.
  • c) Discuss how the categorization of stimuli affects data interpretation.
📝

5. Analyze various Ranking Methods used in psychological measurement.

  • a) Differentiate between simple ranking and comparative ranking.
  • b) Explain how ranking can be used to measure preferences and attitudes.
  • c) Discuss problems such as order effects and respondent fatigue.
📝

6. Explain the use of the Semantic Differential and Q-Sort techniques.

  • a) Describe the structure and interpretation of semantic differential scales.
  • b) Explain how Q-Sort is employed in personality and self-concept research.
  • c) Compare their psychometric strengths and weaknesses.
📝

7. Critically discuss the common Problems and Evaluations of Scaling Methods.

  • a) Identify general challenges faced during scaling (e.g., response bias, social desirability).
  • b) Discuss validity and reliability issues in attitude measurement.
  • c) Suggest strategies to enhance accuracy in psychological scaling.
📝

8. Discuss the Nature and Problems of Rating Scales.

  • a) Describe the psychological basis of rating scales.
  • b) Explain various errors such as the halo effect, leniency, and central tendency.
  • c) Suggest steps to minimize these errors in applied settings.
📝

9. Describe the major Forms of Rating Scales.

  • a) Explain numerical, graphic, descriptive, checklist, and forced-choice formats.
  • b) Discuss their relevance to measurement of attitudes, behavior, and performance.
  • c) Evaluate how scale format choice impacts data reliability and interpretability.
📝

10. Explain selected Tools of Data Collection in psychological research.

  • a) Define sociometric techniques and describe how they reveal group structure and dynamics.
  • b) Outline key projective techniques such as TAT, Rorschach, and sentence completion tests.
📝

11. Define projective techniques and explain their theoretical foundations.

  • a) Discuss how projective tests differ from structured and objective personality measures.
  • b) Explain the role of ambiguity and projection in understanding the unconscious.
  • c) Illustrate with examples how projective tests can reveal hidden motives or conflicts.
📝

12. Discuss the Thematic Apperception Test (TAT) and its use in exploring human motivation.

  • a) Explain the process of stimulus presentation and story construction.
  • b) Describe how themes of achievement, power, and affiliation are analyzed.
  • c) Identify the test’s strengths, limitations, and contemporary adaptations.
📝

13. Examine other major projective techniques used in clinical and research settings.

  • a) Describe the Sentence Completion Test.
  • b) Evaluate the relevance of nonverbal projective tests in cross-cultural assessment.
📝

14. Critically assess the role and current relevance of projective techniques in psychological assessment.

  • a) Discuss the ethical and interpretive concerns associated with their use.
  • b) Compare projective tests with modern self-report and behavioral measures.
  • c) Propose how digital or computer-assisted projective tools could evolve in the future.
📝
